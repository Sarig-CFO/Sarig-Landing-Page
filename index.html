Frequently Asked Questions

What is a fractional CFO?

A fractional CFO is a part-time chief financial officer who provides strategic financial leadership to companies that need CFO-level expertise but aren't ready for a full-time hire. Unlike consultants who advise from the sidelines, a fractional CFO embeds in your team and takes ownership of financial outcomes — from fundraising strategy to monthly closes.

When should a startup hire a fractional CFO?

Israeli startups should consider a fractional CFO when preparing for Series A fundraising, when monthly burn exceeds $50K, when IIA (Israel Innovation Authority) compliance becomes complex, or when the CEO is spending more than 20% of their time on financial operations instead of product and growth.

What's included in fractional CFO services?

SARIG's fractional CFO services include financial modeling and budgeting, monthly close and KPI reporting, cash flow management, fundraising support and data-room preparation, IIA compliance and grant reporting, ERP implementation (Priority), board presentation preparation, and strategic financial advisory.

How is a fractional CFO different from a bookkeeper or accountant?

A bookkeeper records transactions and an accountant ensures compliance, but neither provides strategic financial leadership. A fractional CFO sits at the executive table — building financial models, advising on fundraising strategy, managing investor relations, and making strategic decisions about cash allocation, pricing, and growth planning.

Do you work with pre-revenue startups?

Yes. Pre-revenue startups often have the greatest need for financial structure — building investor-ready models, managing burn rate, preparing for fundraising rounds, and establishing financial controls before scaling. SARIG has experience supporting companies from seed stage through Series A and beyond.

What industries does SARIG specialize in?

SARIG specializes in HealthTech, SaaS, biotech, and medical device companies, with particular expertise in companies operating between Israel and the United States. The dual US-Israel CPA credential enables seamless support for cross-border financial operations, US GAAP compliance, and international subsidiary management.
